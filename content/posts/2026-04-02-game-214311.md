Minos, launching on April 9, redefines the strategy puzzle genre by immersing players in the labyrinthine world of Greek mythology. Instead of merely escaping, players take on the role of the labyrinth itself, tasked with protecting Asterion, the Minotaur, from relentless heroes. This innovative twist not only challenges traditional gameplay but also invites players to engage with the rich narrative of the myth in a unique way.

The games core mechanic revolves around building and controlling the labyrinth, allowing players to manipulate walls, gates, and corridors to thwart intruders. Each decision shapes the maze, creating opportunities to confuse and trap heroes, which adds a layer of strategic depth. With Daedalus as the labyrinth's creator, players will also uncover intriguing lore while feeding the maze with the blood of fallen adventurers, enhancing the immersive experience.

As players embark on repeatable runs, they will encounter new traps, enemies, and layouts each night, ensuring that no two experiences are the same. This dynamic gameplay not only keeps the challenge fresh but also encourages players to experiment with different strategies. Will you be able to protect the Minotaur and master the labyrinth?
