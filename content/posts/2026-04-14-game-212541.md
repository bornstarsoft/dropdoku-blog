In a recent internal memo, Asha Sharma, the new head of Xbox, revealed that Game Pass has become "too expensive" for players, indicating potential changes to the subscription service's pricing structure. This statement comes just six months after Microsoft raised the price of its top-tier Game Pass subscription by a staggering 50%, from $20 to $30 per month. Sharma emphasized the need for a "better value equation" to ensure the service remains attractive to gamers.

As it stands, Xbox Game Pass offers several tiers, including Ultimate, Premium, Essential, and PC Game Pass, each with varying price points. The price hikes were reportedly influenced by the inclusion of blockbuster titles like Call of Duty, which cost Microsoft significant revenue. With these changes on the horizon, gamers are left wondering how Microsoft will adapt Game Pass to meet their needs without sacrificing quality or access to popular games.

As the gaming landscape continues to evolve, it will be interesting to see how Microsoft balances pricing with value. Will the changes to Game Pass enhance the overall gaming experience, or will they lead to further dissatisfaction among players? Only time will tell.
