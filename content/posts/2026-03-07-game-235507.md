In a significant turn of events, Scottish artist Fern Hook has been credited as a visual design consultant in Bungie's recently released game, Marathon, after her artwork was used without permission during the game's alpha phase. This incident highlights the critical importance of proper attribution and ethical practices in the gaming industry, especially as it continues to evolve and expand.

The resolution of this issue, which involved Bungie acknowledging the unauthorized use of Hook's work and taking steps to rectify the situation, serves as a reminder for developers to respect the creative rights of artists. As the gaming community becomes more aware of these ethical considerations, it raises questions about how studios can better integrate artists into their processes and ensure fair compensation for their contributions.

As we move forward, it will be interesting to see how this incident influences industry standards and practices regarding artistic collaboration. Will more companies follow suit in recognizing and compensating artists for their work?
